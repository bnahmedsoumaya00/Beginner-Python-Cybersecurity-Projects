Protection Methods

Defend Yourself Against Phishing

Implement these strategies to protect your credentials and personal information

Disable or Limit Autofill

Chrome

Settings → Autofill → Passwords → Turn off "Offer to save passwords"

Firefox

Settings → Privacy & Security → Logins and Passwords → Uncheck autofill options

Safari

Preferences → AutoFill → Uncheck "User names and passwords"

Edge

Settings → Profiles → Passwords → Turn off "Offer to save passwords"

⚠️ Note: Disabling autofill reduces convenience but significantly increases security

Use a Password Manager

Password managers verify domains before autofilling, protecting against phishing.

Recommended Options:

1Password

Cross-platform, family plans, secure vault

Bitwarden

Open-source, free tier, self-hosting option

LastPass

User-friendly, business features

Dashlane

VPN included, dark web monitoring

Why Password Managers Help:

  • Domain Verification: Only fills on exact domain matches
  • Strong Passwords: Generates unique, complex passwords
  • Encrypted Storage: Protects your credentials
  • Audit Features: Identifies weak/reused passwords
  • Breach Alerts: Notifies if credentials are compromised

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Even if phishing captures your password, 2FA provides a second layer of protection.

2FA Methods (Ranked by Security):

1. Hardware Security Keys (Best)

YubiKey, Google Titan - Physical devices, phishing-resistant

2. Authenticator Apps (Good)

Google Authenticator, Authy, Microsoft Authenticator - TOTP codes

3. SMS Codes (Acceptable)

Better than nothing, but vulnerable to SIM swapping

4. Email Codes (Weakest)

If email is compromised, 2FA is bypassed

Enable 2FA On:

  • Email accounts (Gmail, Outlook, etc.)
  • Financial services (banks, PayPal, Venmo)
  • Social media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram)
  • Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud)
  • Work accounts (Office 365, Slack, etc.)
  • Password managers
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges

Verify URLs Carefully

Always check the URL before entering credentials.

What to Look For:

Legitimate URL

https://accounts.google.com/signin
  • ✅ Correct domain (google.com)
  • ✅ HTTPS with valid certificate
  • ✅ No typos or extra characters

Phishing URLs

https://accounts-google.com/signin https://google.com.secure-login.net https://goog1e.com/signin
  • ❌ Typos (goog1e instead of google)
  • ❌ Extra subdomains
  • ❌ Different TLD (.net instead of .com)

Remember

Security is a layered approach. No single method is perfect, but combining multiple protection strategies significantly reduces your risk. Stay vigilant, verify everything, and when in doubt, don't click!
