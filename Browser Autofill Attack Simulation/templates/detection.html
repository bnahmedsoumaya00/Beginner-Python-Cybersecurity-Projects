How to Detect Phishing Attacks

Spot the Red Flags

Learn to identify phishing attempts before they compromise your security

Method 1: URL Analysis

The URL is your first line of defense. Always examine it carefully.

Red Flags in URLs:

  • Typosquatting: gooogle.com, paypa1.com, arnaz0n.com
  • Subdomain tricks: google.com.phishing-site.net
  • Wrong TLD: facebook.net (should be .com)

Method 2: SSL Certificate Verification

Legitimate sites use valid SSL certificates. Always check them.

  1. Click the padlock icon in the address bar
  2. View certificate details
  3. Verify domain name matches exactly
  4. Check issuing authority

Method 3: Email Analysis

Phishing often starts with email. Watch for:

  • Urgency/threats ("Account will be closed!")
  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
  • Spelling and grammar errors
  • Suspicious links and attachments
  • Requests for passwords or personal info

Quick Detection Checklist

Use this before entering credentials:

  • ☐ Verified the URL is EXACTLY correct
  • ☐ Checked SSL certificate is valid
  • ☐ No spelling or grammatical errors
  • ☐ Design looks professional
  • ☐ Not feeling rushed or pressured

If ANY checkbox is unchecked, DO NOT enter credentials!
